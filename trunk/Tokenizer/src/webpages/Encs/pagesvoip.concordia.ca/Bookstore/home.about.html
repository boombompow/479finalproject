About the Concordia
University Bookstore

An important part of the community here at Concordia University (Montreal, Quebec, Canada), the "Mission" of the Bookstore and Computer Store is to operate as a financially self-supporting retail operation that provides support services which assist the University Community to achieve its primary goals of education, research and community service.

The Bookstore carries new and used textbooks, Custom CourseWare, reference and general books, stationery and office supplies, crested products and confectionery items. The Bookstore also purchases and sells textbooks for Marianopolis College, Queen of Angels Academy, and North Star Academy.

As a founding member of SellMyTextbooks.org the Concordia Bookstore also buys back used textbooks from students for resale. Since Summer 2010, we have also been offering a Textbook Rental program, and also offer eBooks and Online Access (PIN) Codes for download.

The Bookstore operates two stores on a year-round basis. One store is situated on the Loyola Campus in the Communication Studies and Journalism Building (CJ) in N.D.G., and one store in the Sir George Williams Campus' Library Building (LB) in downtown Montreal (click for campus maps).

The Computer Store offers hardware and software at educational pricing as well as a full range of peripherals. The Computer Store is accredited with the Ministry of Education thus enabling the store to accept student loans for personal computer purchases.

The Computer Store is located on the SGW campus (Hall Building - Mezzanine Level), and they have a Loyola campus store located within the Loyola Bookstore in the CJ Building and a Service/Repair Center in the Loyola CC Building. For more information regarding the Computer Store, please visit their CCS web site.

The Digital Store (formerly Printing Services) operates a copy center at the SGW campus (Webster Library - LB - Building, ground floor), and a Digital Boutique store in the LB Building which offers a variety of electronics and accessories. There is also a copy center within the Loyola Bookstore. The Digital Store also maintains the University's copier fleet.


All of the Campus Retail Stores are owned and operated by Concordia University.


The Concordia University Bookstore is a member of the National Association of College Stores,   Campus Stores Canada, and of the Canadian Campus Retail Associates (click for list of other member stores).

The Concordia University Bookstore is proud to be a three-time nominee of the Canadian Book Publishers Council and was named winner of the Bookseller of the Year Award in 1993.

The Concordia University Bookstore received "Honorable Mention" in 1993 by the Canadian Association of University Business Officers for the creation of the " Customized Student Booklist" program.

Concordia University Bookstore is proud to have received the 1999 Ecogold Maple Leaf Award for Outstanding Achievement in support of Canadian Manufacturers.
