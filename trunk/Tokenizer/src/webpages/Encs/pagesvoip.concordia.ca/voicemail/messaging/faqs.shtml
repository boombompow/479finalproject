Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ's – UNITY CONNECTION VOICE-MAIL SYSTEM

Q. What is my Cisco ID?
A. Your Cisco ID is your four-digit telephone extension.

Q. What is my PIN?
A. Your PIN is the same as your voice-mail password.

Q. I have forgotten my PIN. How can I reset my PIN?
A. Contact the IITS Helpline and request a PIN reset. The IITS Helpline can be reached at extension 7613 or at help@concordia.ca .

Q. I'm trying to set a new PIN but the system keeps rejecting it, saying it is invalid?
A. In order to be accepted by the system your PIN must follow the guidelines described at http://voip.concordia.ca/voicemail/messaging/setup.shtml.

Q. What number do I dial to access voice-mail from my office telephone?
A. You can reach the voice-mail system from your office phone by pressing the message key or by dialling 4220.

Q. How do I access voice-mail from off-campus?
A. Dial 514-848-4220. When you hear the prompt: “Welcome to Concordia University's Unified Messaging. If you have a voice mailbox on this system, please press star”, press the * key and enter your ID (four digit telephone extension), followed by #. Enter your PIN (voice-mail password) followed by #.

Q. Where can I find a description of the prompts used by the system?
A. The prompts are virtually the same. To get a copy of the updated voice-mail map, click here: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/guides/Voice-mail_UserGuide_8.0.pdf .

Q. I am a Microsoft Exchange user. How can I check my voice-mail in my email client on the new voice-mail system?
A. Use the Internet Message Access Protocol (IMAP) service. IMAP is the closest to what was offered to Microsoft Exchanges users on the old voice-mail system. You can access email and voice-mail messages and play and delete voice messages from within the same desktop email client using the built-in ViewMail for Outlook. For instructions on how to set up your IMAP, visit our website: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml .

Q. What is the IMAP service?
A. Internet Message Access Protocol (IMAP) gives users another option to access their voice-mail in addition to the telephone. It is a service close to what was offered to Microsoft Exchange users on the old voice-mail system. Your voice-mail appears as an email message attachment in your email client. Users can also compose, reply to, and forward messages by using IMAP clients. Users who wish to take advantage of this service will need to set up their email client software to access their voice-mail as an IMAP email account. Various standards-based desktop email clients are supported, including Microsoft Outlook, Mac Mail and Entourage for Mac. Instructions on how to set-up your IMAP are available on our website: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml .

Q. What is the PCA Messaging Inbox?
A. Cisco Personal Communications Assistant (PCA) Message Inbox is a tool that allows you to listen to your voice-mail messages over the web. All of the voice-mail messages you receive are collected into an inbox that is accessible through your internet browser. By selecting the controls within the Messaging Inbox, you can make and play voice-mail recordings with either your phone or your computer microphone and speakers. For instructions on how to access your PCA, visit our website: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml .

Q. What is the website where I can access my PCA Messaging Inbox?
A. https://10.100.1.28/ciscopca You can safely click 'Yes' in response to any security alerts that may appear as you use this website. The Cisco PCA website uses pop-up windows. If you have pop-up blocker software installed, configure it to enable pop-ups for this site.

Q. What is the username and password for my Cisco PCA?
A. Your MyConcordia portal netname and password. If these login credentials do not work, contact the IITS Helpline at extension 7613 or help@concordia.ca .

Q. On the old voice-mail system, I could view and listen to my voice-mail messages using my Blackberry. How can I do this on the new voice-mail system?
A. While on campus, Blackberry users can access their voice-mail messages using the Cisco Personal Communications Assistant (PCA) inbox via this link: https://10.100.1.28/ciscopca . For complete instructions on how to access this service, visit our website: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml .

Q. How can I access the Cisco PCA inbox off campus?
A. You can access the Cisco PCA inbox off-campus at the same link (https://10.100.1.28/ciscopca) but it can only be accessed through the Concordia Virtual Private Network (VPN) connection. To learn how to use the Concordia VPN connection, please visit this website: http://helpline.concordia.ca/network/vpn/ . For additional assistance with setting up your VPN connection, contact the IITS Helpline at help@concordia.ca or extension 7613.

 

FAQ's – VoIP TELEPHONE SYSTEM

Q. How what is my username and password for the Cisco Call Manager User (CCM)?
A. Your MyConcordia Portal netname and your MyConcordia portal password.

Q. What is the website where I can access the CCM User from outside the portal?
A. https://10.100.1.23/ccmuser You can safely click “Yes” in response to any security alerts that may appear as you use the CCM User website.

Q. My 7960 telephone set is broken and I require a replacement. Can I just order a new 7960 telephone?
A. To order a replacement telephone, contact your telephone VOIP coordinator. A list of telephone VoIP coordinators can be found here: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/whotocontact/coordinators/cord2.html. Cisco 7960 model telephones are at the end of service and are no longer being produced by Cisco. The 7960 model is being replaced by the 7962 model. To download the manual for the new telephone models, click here: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml.

Q. I want a new 7940 telephone for new employee coming to our department. Who do I contact to place the order?
A. Speak with the telephone VoIP coordinator. VoIP coordinators can be found here: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/whotocontact/coordinators/cord2.html. You cannot order a new 7940 telephone because they are no longer being produced by Cisco. The 7940 model is being replaced by the 7942 model. To download the manual for the new telephone models, click here:http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml.

Q. I want a new 7920 wireless telephone for a new employee coming to our department. Who do I contact to place the order?
A. Speak with the telephone VoIP coordinator. VoIP coordinators can be found here: http://voip.concordia.ca/help/whotocontact/coordinators/cord2.html. You cannot order a new 7920 wireless telephone because they are no longer being produced by Cisco. The 7920 model is being replaced by the 7921 model. To download the manual for the new telephone models, click here:http://voip.concordia.ca/help/refmaterials/index2.shtml.
