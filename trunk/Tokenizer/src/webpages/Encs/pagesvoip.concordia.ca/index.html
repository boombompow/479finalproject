E-mail phishing attacks

In the wake of several phishing scams at Concordia (please see latest below), we wish to remind everyone to please delete any such e-mails immediately.

Phishers send out mass e-mails to targeted recipients with the hopes of at least a few recipients replying with their personal information. Unfortunately, Concordia is often targeted due to the large number of account holders.

Concordia University will never ask for your personal information by phone, e-mail, or URL unless it is in response to a support request you have initiated. Concordia University will never ask for your password, nor should businesses, banks, and other institutions. Please take the time to read our e-mail phishing FAQ available at: http://helpline.concordia.ca/email/scams.

If you clicked on the URL or replied to the e-mail with your username and password, please change your Alcor and netname passwords immediately by following these steps:

• Log into your MyConcordia portal at: http://myconcordia.ca.
• Click on "Personal Services" in the main menu followed by"Computer Accounts & Passwords".
• Under "Existing personal accounts" click on your Alcor and netname accounts.
• You will then have the option to enter a new secure password.

If you are ever unsure of the authenticity of an e-mail, or if you have any other questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to contact the Helpline at (514) 848-2424, ext. 7613 or help@concordia.ca.

ATTENTION: Beware of technical support phone scam

Several members of the Concordia community have been randomly targeted by callers pretending to be from Microsoft, Windows Support, HP, etc. phoning to fix a presumed computer problem. Many calls have originated from 1-253-802-0308, although the phone number may vary.

The caller advises potential victims that their computer has been flagged by their service provider and asks that special instructions be followed to remedy the situation. This allows the caller to trick people into installing malicious software on their computer, or even take remote control over the computer, thereby leaving it vulnerable.

IITS wishes to remind the Concordia community to ignore any such calls. IITS' desktop support contracts and campus agreements with Microsoft and HP will never result in users receiving calls to their office or home directly from the manufacturer. These calls can be hazardous for your computer.

If you are ever unsure of the authenticity of a call, please do not hesitate to contact the IITS Helpline at help@concordia.ca or (514) 848-2424, ext. 7613. Thank you.

New Sophos package (Home Edition) available June 23

All those who have downloaded Sophos Anti-Virus software through the MyConcordia portal and installed it on a home computer are asked to download the software again before June 30, 2011. This will ensure that users continue to receive the virus signature file updates from Sophos.


The new Sophos package is available to download from the MyConcordia portal under Personal Services > Sophos Anti-Virus.

After June 30, 2011, the virus signature file updates for Sophos Anti-Virus will expire. The Sophos software will remain functional, however, updates will cease unless users download the new software package.

The notice regarding the expiry of these updates was included in the Sophos software agreement form that appeared before users downloaded Sophos from the MyConcordia portal. Please note this expiry date does not affect Sophos Anti-Virus installations on Concordia work computers.

For questions or concerns, please contact the IITS Helpline at help@concordia.ca or extension 7613.
