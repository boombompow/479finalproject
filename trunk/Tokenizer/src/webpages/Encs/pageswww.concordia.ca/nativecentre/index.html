WELCOME!

 
 


Welcome to the Centre for Native Education’s website.  In the following pages, you will find information on our services, programmes and activities as well as events that are of interest to First Nations, Métis and Inuit students.

Whether you are a currently registered student or you are thinking of studying at Concordia, the important thing to remember is that we are here to help, support and encourage you to achieve your highest academic potential. 

The Centre for Native Education is your home away from home, where all First Nations, Métis and Inuit students can work and grow together in a community environment that embraces Native cultures.

The Centre for Native Education is devoted to helping First Nations, Métis and Inuit students at Concordia University achieve their highest academic potential.

To this end, the Centre for Native Education:

  • Provides direct services and programmes which promote the academic, personal growth, and holistic development of First Nations, Métis and Inuit students.
  • Encourages the academic and administrative units of Concordia University to provide services that meet the needs of Aboriginal students, improve the quality of their experience, and promote their well-being.
  • Networks and maintains good relations with other Aboriginal service organizations to promote, and better meet the interests and needs of Aboriginal students.
  • Promotes the benefits of a post-secondary education to potential Aboriginal students.
  • Acts as a resource centre on Aboriginal cultures, languages, history and contemporary issues.

Graduate Scholarships

The CNE is proud to announce the following Graduate Scholarships which are being offered this year again.

  1. ONEXONE Foundation Graduate Scholarship:  The purpose of the award is to encourage graduate students from First Peoples, Métis and Inuit communities, recognized as such by the University's Centre for Native Education who are currently enrolled at Concordia.  The Scholarship is to be awarded on the basis of academic excellence taking into consideration the candidates transcripts, experience, research potential, statement of purpose and letter of recommendation.  It is valued at $7,500 for one year.  Candidates may reapply in subsequent years and be considered in the pool of applicants. The Scholarship is only tenable within the first three years of a master's program and within the first five years of a doctoral program.

  2. Concordia Student Support Graduate Scholarship:  The purpose of the award is to encourage graduate students from First Peoples, Métis and Inuit communities, recognized as such by the University's Centre for Native Education who are currently enrolled at Concordia. Candidates must be registered full-time in a master's or doctoral program.  The Scholarship is to be awarded on the basis of academic excellence taking into consideration the candidates transcripts, experience, research potential, statement of purpose and letter of recommendation. The award is valued at $5,000 for one year.  The scholarship is only tenable within the first three years of a master's and within the first five years of a doctoral program.  Recipients may reapply in subsequent years and be considered in the pool of applicants as long as they are within the time limit of the award being tenable.

Application Deadline: October 21, 2011 at 5 pm at the CNE Office.

You must provide a complete application which consists of:

  1. Unofficial transcripts of all university studies (bachelor, master, doctoral);
  2. An up-to-date C.V. including a list of all publications and relevant professional and research experience;
  3. A statement of purpose indicating the work you have done in your program and your goals for the upcoming academic year (maximum 500 words);
  4. One letter of recommendation from your supervisor or a faculty member who has taught you.

Doris Orr (Morning Star): Elder.
Born in Fort McKay, Alberta.

Morning Star is from the Cree nation and a mother of four. For the past 25 years she has dedicated her life to care-taking Native wisdom. Morning Star was initiated by her Elders to share and teach cultural and spiritual knowledge to benefit all our Relations,. As a speaker and a guide to many, she is a keeper of the Full Moon Teachings for women and directs Healing Circles for all people of all races.

Students are welcome to drop by the Centre to meet with morning star. She is available for group or individual consultation.
