about us

 
 


Darryl Martin:
Undergraduate student in Civil Engineering

My name is Darryl Martin. I am a Mohawk from Akwesasne.  I am a husband, a father to four children, and a grandfather. At the age of forty, I decided to go back to university after finishing another career as a horse shoer. I am completing my Civil Engineering Bachelor program this semester. I am presently working for the Mohawk Bridge Consortium's Mercier Bridge Rehabilitation effort. The role I assume is to help the project manager analyze and implement resources.

Concordia’s Centre for Native Education has provided a tremendous amount of support to my academic endeavors. They have provided me with resources that I needed to excel at my degree. The informal atmosphere contributes to relaxed but productive surroundings with many benefits.

SCHOLARSHIP WINNERS


Lauren McComber

This summer, 26-year-old journalism student student Lauren McComber - a Mohawk from the Kahnawake First Nation - won the prestigious Ted Nolan Rose Nolan Memorial Scholarship Fund. Out of 200 applicants for the Rose Nolan Scholarship Fund, only 10 aboriginal women were chosen. Lauren also won the Strategic Alliance of Broadcasters for Aboriginal Reflection (SABAR) Scholarship, for which she was the sole recipient.

Both scholarships awarded 5,000.00 to each winner.

Lauren was also accepted into the the first ever Indigenous Women in Community Leadership (IWCL) program offered by the Coady International Institute at St. Francis Xavier University in Antigonish, Nova Scotia. Out of 65 applicants, only 12 Aboriginal woman from across Canada were chosen to take part in the three-week program offered from May 2 - May 20th 2011.

Upon completion of the course, the women went back to their communities and implemented what they learned in the IWCL program. Lauren put together a focus group called 'Re-visioning the Kanien'kehá:ka Woman', which explored Kanien'kehá:ka (Mohawk) women's traditional values, roles and traits to see how they blend with modern day non-Native women's values, roles and traits in order to strengthen their identity and build their self-esteem.

The 'Re-visioning the Kanien'kehá:ka Woman' focus group is now in the process of becoming Kahnawake's first Young Women's group.
Currently, Lauren works for Kahnawake's first TV Station The CKER as the host of the CKER News. She will be applying for her second major in First Peoples Studies when the program becomes available, and is expected to graduate in the fall semester of 2013.

Megan Kanerahtenha: wi Whyte
Please see Artwork

Megan Kanerahtenha: wi Whyte is a Mohawk youth of Kahnawake working to voice the issues of her community to the outside world. She is completing a BA in Art Education major at Concordia University and will pursue a MA in Art Therapy. Her Art focuses on the journey of creation and the moments of self discovery experienced along the way.  She states that “Art is about living life and striving to understand our role in the universe. With my work in one hand and my heart in the other, I wish to teach the world about my culture, my history, and my future as a First Nation's woman. I want to break the stereotype simply by living my life in my art practice. I dream to create in other native communities across Canada, helping all people become artists".

Visit her website for more information: http://kanerahtenhawi.deviantart.com/
