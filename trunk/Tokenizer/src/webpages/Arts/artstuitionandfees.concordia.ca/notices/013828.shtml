The University Writing Test: Questions and Answers for students

Effective immediately, the University’s Writing Skills Requirement is being waived for all Undergraduate students, with the exception of students enrolled in the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science.

We’ve compiled some questions and answers to help you understand how this might affect you:

1. I had to take extra credits as a result of failing the University Writing Test (UWT). Will I receive any refund for these extra courses?

No. The requirement was in place at the time the UWT was written, and the discontinuation of the UWT does not affect that.

2. My graduation was delayed because of an earlier failure to satisfy the UWT requirement. Will I be compensated for the delay?

No. Students in these circumstances will now be able to graduate without further delay, provided they have satisfied all other degree requirements.

3. If there is no new regulation in place, why will the students who are admitted in the up-coming sessions not be required to meet a Writing Skills Requirement? Is this fair?

The University Writing Skills Requirements is under review. In the meantime, all university students must still meet their own program’s writing skills requirements where applicable.

4. So writing skills are not important anymore?

On the contrary. In fact, the University believes that the UWT was not effectively measuring what it believes are other core skills (including not only written but oral communication, presentation skills, critical thinking and problem-solving, and numeracy among others). The Provost’s Working Group on Teaching and Learning will examine the question of core skills acquisition and will present recommendations on their findings.

5. If the previous test was not the right one, why did it take the University so long to see this? Why doesn’t the University require it anymore?

There are many reasons. The University has been discussing every aspect of the University Writing Test and its implementation for some time now. As a result of this research, the University has determined that the UWT is not as effective as it could be. Since many students are able to pass all degree requirements before taking the UWT, while others fail the UWT after passing all other requirements it suggests that the test is not achieving the result for which it was originally written. The University would like to make sure that any new test is appropriate for the entire Concordia community before implementing any changes.

6. Is the English Placement Test also abolished?

No. the English Placement Test is unaffected by this recommendation.

7. What’s going to happen to my transcript?

Your student record (the record which you can access through your MyConcordia portal) will still include the information about the University Writing Skills Requirement. However, when you apply to graduate, your record will be updated so that it will read ‘University Writing Skills Requirement : Not Required’. In the interim, you do not need to be concerned that this aspect of your record is not up to date. This is to prevent the natural delays which would occur if we were to update the records of all students at once.

8. I am registered in ENGL 212 (or FRAA 413) right now (in the Fall semester 2008). Can I drop this course now that the UWT has been suspended?

No you cannot (not for the Fall semester of 2008), as the deadlines have already passed and the course has been completed. In most cases, these courses can be used as electives and can be used to fulfill the requirement to complete 24 credits outside your major program. You should verify this with your academic advisor.

9. I am registered in ENGL 212 (or FRAA 413) for January 2009 to satisfy the UWT requirements. Can I now drop this course and choose another one?

Yes. However, we strongly recommend that you do not drop this course as it will help you improve your writing skills. Please be aware that many courses are already full and you might not be able to register for an alternative course. You may be able to use this course as one of your non-program electives. This should be verified with your academic advisor.


For more information, see the notice The University Writing Test has been suspended.
