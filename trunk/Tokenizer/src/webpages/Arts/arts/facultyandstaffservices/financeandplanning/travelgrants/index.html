Travel grants

Local tools

  •  
  •  
  • Printable version
 

Conference travel grants are available. If you are interested in receiving a travel grant, you can assess whether or not you are eligible below.

Who is eligible?

Individuals holding tenured, tenure-track, and ETA positions in the Faculty of Arts and Science are eligible for travel funding in any given fiscal year for one (1) scholarly conference.

What are the criteria?

  • You must be on Payroll at the time of the conference.
  • You must not be on sabbatical at the time of the conference.
  • You must be presenting a paper or poster, giving a keynote address, chairing a session, or participating formally in some other major fashion.
  • You must seek prior written approval from your Chair for an absence of less than a week if you are teaching. For an absence of more than a week, you must seek prior written approval from the Dean.
  • You must submit the expense claim form, duly filled out and approved by your department head, within two weeks of returning from the conference.
  • You must respect the University Travel Policy.

Where can I go?

The amount funded, ranging from $400 to $1,000, will depend upon your conference location. Travel grants are generally awarded as follows:

  • $400 Canada (less than 500 km from Montreal, including Ottawa, Quebec City, & Toronto)
  • $600 Canada (more than 500 km from Montreal)
  • $750 U.S.A., Mexico, and Caribbean
  • $1,000 Other International Destinations

When can I go?

The last date of the conference must be between May 1 and April 30. For any conference with an end date of May 31 or later, you must apply for funding in the academic year.

Why provide funding at all?

Travel grants are provided to help offset the cost of conference travel of faculty members, and to raise the international profile of Concordia University's Faculty of Arts and Science.

How do I get a travel grant?

You no longer apply in advance to the Dean’s Office. A faculty member who is eligible for travel funding will simply have to complete an expense claim form upon their return from a scholarly conference, have the form approved by their departmental Chair, and forward it to the Faculty’s Finance and Planning Office in L-AD 532 along with appropriate receipts.

Travel Expense Reimbursement

In order to be reimbursed for your travel expenses, please fill out an expense claim form and submit it along with your original receipts to your budget assistant.

All travel expenses reimbursed by the University must be in accordance with the University Travel Policy. If the travel policy is not followed, Financial Services will not reimburse your expenses. For reimbursement of airfare or train fare, please note that the original boarding pass is required.

A Travel Voucher must be completed once flight arrangements have been made with one of the University's travel agents. The Travel Voucher must be submitted to the Finance and Planning Office, AD-532, Loyola, for processing. We will forward the completed travel voucher to Financial Services. Please note that tickets will not be released by the travel agent until an approved Travel Voucher has been received.
