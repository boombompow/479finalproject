Current Students

PLEASE NOTE THAT IT IS THE RESPONSIBILITY OF ALL CURRENT STUDENTS IN THE FACULTY TO REGISTER FOR FALL AND WINTER COURSES PRIOR TO AUGUST AND REPORT ANY PROBLEMS TO STUDENT ACADEMIC SERVICES.

Failure to register for both terms prior to August may result in an unfavorable schedule or in extreme cases the unavailability of required courses.

Please read the following guidelines for assistance with registration:

  1. All readmitted students are required to attend a readmission workshop prior to being allowed to register.
     
  2. All students should be following the appropriate course sequence for their program. Sequences are located at the Student Academic Services Office website. If your sequence is not available or you encounter any problems regarding the selection of courses because you are off-sequence, please contact Student Academic Services for further assistance.
     
  3. Please note that your sequences were designed to ensure that you can graduate within the regular program length. Failure to follow the recommended sequence may result in time conflicts in later sessions and may delay your graduation.
     
  4. Please note that all requests for course substitutions must be submitted to Student Academic Services at least 1 month prior to the start of a term. Requests should NOT be submitted directly to a department.
     
  5. Students in a Bachelor of Computer Science program who have been admitted or readmitted prior to the 2009-2010 academic year, should follow the academic calendar for the year to which they have been admitted /readmitted.
     
  6. Students in a Bachelor of Computer Science program who have been admitted or readmitted after the 2009-2010 academic year, should refer to section 71.20.6 of the Undergraduate Student Calendar located at: registrar.concordia.ca/calendar/pdf/sec71.pdf in order to find the list of General Electives for Engineering and Computer Science.
     
  7. No students in the faculty may take COMP 201, INTE 290 and MATH 200, 208 or 209, for credit.
     
  8. Students are urged to register according to the registration start dates. No special arrangements will be made if you delay registration.
     
  9. Alternate week Laboratory schedule:
    Fall Term: Labs are scheduled to commence September 12th for week (1) labs and September 19th for week (2) labs.
    Winter Term: Labs are scheduled to commence January 9th for week (1) labs and January 16th for week (2) labs.
     
  10. All students must have completed all “200” level courses required for their program before they can register for any “400” level course.



Students in Co-operative Programs
Co-op students will be given priority for registration. If you are currently a Co-op student you may register on the first day of registration for each of the Summer and Fall/Winter terms. It is your responsibility to register during this time period to ensure that you are given priority.



 Advising and Registration for Newly Admitted Students — September and January Admissions
As a newly admitted student in ENCS, you are highly encouraged to attend one of the orientation sessions that are organized by the Faculty. However you do not require advising in order to register. Once you have been accepted, you can register immediately on the Portal. All details are enclosed with your acceptance package or may be obtained online at the Student Academic Services Office website. To schedule your group advising session you must call (514) 848-2424 ext. 3055

New Students to the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science, including students who have recently been granted a degree transfer, should:

  1. Read the information provided in your acceptance package from the Office of the Registrar, including the information provided to you by the Faculty.
     

  2. Book your advising session.
     

  3. Please make sure you read the Advising Guide for new students available in early April, prior to registering. Should you experience any problems, please contact the Student Academic Services Office.
     

  4. Students must register for both the Fall, and Winter terms. Failure to do so may result in an inappropriate schedule or inability to take required courses according to your sequence.
     

  5. International Students and students from out-of-province should refer to the information enclosed in their acceptance package.

    Should you experience any problems, please contact:
    Sonia McCalla, (514) 848-2424 ext. 3055, (sonia_mccalla@encs.concordia.ca) or Salvatore Colavita, (514) 848-2424 ext. 3254 (sal@encs.concordia.ca) for further assistance with registration.



Out-of-Faculty Students
Students not enrolled in a Computer Science program may register for a maximum of 8 Computer Science credits per term and may not take more than 30 credits of COMP courses numbered higher than COMP 212.



 Independent Students
Independent Students who wish to take courses in the Faculty will need to have the required prerequisite completed prior to taking the course.



 Visiting Students
Visiting Students must be in good standing at their home institution and currently registered in an Engineering or Computer Science program. Students on probation (failed or conditional standing) at their home institution will not be considered as visiting or independent students.

Requests from Visiting Students to take courses in the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Priority for all courses will be to students currently enrolled in the Faculty of Engineering and Computer Science at Concordia University.

Individuals mandated to take courses by a professional organization, such as the Ordre des ingénieurs du Québec or the Ordre des architects, must submit a letter to Student Academic Services, from the organization indicating the courses that are recommended.

For further information on visiting students (including from professional organizations), please consult the Student Academic Services Office.



Summer Registration

You are permitted to register for a maximum of 12 credits, with no more than 6 credits in each summer term, with the exception of the following:

  1. Co-op students following the sequence recommended by their Department

  2. Students entering the Faculty in the proceeding January session who are required to take a full-time summer course load.

  3. Potential graduates who require a course to remain in sequence.

The 12 credit rule also applies to out-of-Faculty courses.

Students wishing to take a full-course load who do not meet Item 1 or 2 must submit a written student request form to the Student Academic Services Office to be permitted to take more credits. Approval of course overloads during the summer is based on a GPA of 3.00 or higher, and proximity to graduation.
