Executive Committee

 

President

Veronique Tokateloff, BEng 05, MASc 08

Véronique Tokateloff obtained her Masters of Applied Science in mechanical engineering at the Royal Military College of Canada in Kingston, Ontario, and completed her thesis work at the Canadian Space Agency in St. Hubert, Quebec. In 2005, she received her Bachelor of Engineering degree (mechanical/aerospace) from Concordia University. In 2002-2003, she was the only undergraduate student in the faculty of Engineering and Computer Science to participate in a one-year international academic exchange: she attended the prestigious École Nationale Supérieure de l'Aéronautique et de l'Espace in Toulouse, France.

Véronique has always strongly believed that a proper education reaches far beyond the confines of books and classrooms. During her undergraduate degree, Véronique was greatly involved in the Engineering and Computer Science Students' Association (ECA), on the Engineering Games executive and as a member of SAE AeroDesign. She was elected Vice-President of External Affairs for the 2003-2004 mandate. Her involvement later earned her the Engineering and Computer Science Council on Student Life Award.

Since January 2008, Véronique has been working in the space and automotive industries as a technical consultant and structural analyst at Maya Heat Transfer Technologies. She is also the proud mother of baby boy, born in January 2011.

Véronique's experience with Concordia's ECA and the industry have enabled her to develop leadership and project-implementation skills that will prove beneficial to the Engineering and Computer Science Alumni Chapter (ECAC). As President of the ECAC, she wishes to help alumni extend their Concordia experience beyond their diplomas and to keep them passionate about their field and in touch with their alma mater! Véronique enjoys collaborating with the ECAC executive to help the chapter achieve its goals through strategic programming, outreach and networking activities.

 

Vice-President

Ali Boroumandian, BCompSc 08

Ali holds a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science with a minor in Information Systems from Concordia University. He is currently pursuing his Master's in Software Engineering at Concordia.

Ali has worked many years as an entrepreneur. He has work experience in sales, management, marketing, consulting, and in computer technology. He plans to assist the ECAC to promote job and networking related opportunities to alumni.

 

Secretary

Claude Fachkha, MEng 10, PhD candidate

Claude Fachkha earned a BEng in Computer and Communication Engineering from Notre Dame University in 2008, then  enrolled at Concordia and completed a Master’s of Engineering in Information Systems Security with high distinction two years later. After receiving a four-year scholarship, he’s continuing his studies at Concordia as a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering.

Claude has been a teaching assistant for graduate courses, and  he is currently working as a research assistant under the supervision of Prof. Mourad Debbabi on various research projects. Since 2008 he’s also been a member of the IT helpdesk, providing tech support for students and faculty members. He would like to share his expertise and help the ECAC attain success through networking, management and operation.

 

Event Coordinator

Khaled Murywed, BEng 08

After graduating in 2008, with his degree in Electrical Engineering, Khaled Murywed was faced with the challenge of finding a job—a stressful situation for many graduates embarking upon their careers.

Now that he has a full-time job in the telecommunications industry, he feels compelled to provide job search tools to new alumni by facilitating their introduction to the job market. He hopes to accomplish this task by organizing events which will encourage networking and socializing, both efficient methods to find employment. Alumni are welcome to approach Khaled at any ECAC event if they are seeking career advice.

 

IT Coordinator

Arash Ashkan Alam, MASc 10

Arash Ashkan Alam received his Bachelors in Engineering, with a minor in industrial safety, from Iran University of Science and Technology in Tehran. He subsequently earned his Masters in Industrial Engineering at Concordia, with a major in supply chain management. He currently works in the aerospace industry in project management. Arash is happy to share his interview strategies with ECAC members.

 

Immediate Past Vice-President

Aditya Sharma, BEng 03

Aditya Sharma graduated in 2003 in Mechanical Engineering from Concordia University. Shortly after graduating and running a number of businesses, he switched career paths and became a Financial Security Advisor. He has been at Investors Group for the past three years, helping clients achieve their financial dreams (for today and tomorrow).

He also has other affiliations with organizations such as Business Networking International (B.N.I). Aditya is a football coach with the St. Leonard Cougars Jr. Program.
