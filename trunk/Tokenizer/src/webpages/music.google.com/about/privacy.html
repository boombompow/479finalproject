Privacy Policy

Last modified: November 16, 2011

The Google Privacy Policy describes how we treat personal information when you use Google's products and services, including information provided when you use Google Music Storage, the Google Music Store, the Google Music Software, Google Music Social Recommendations, and the Google Music Artist Hub, each of which is defined in the Additional Terms of Service for Google Music ("Google Music Terms") and each is a Service as defined in Section 1.1 of the Google Terms of Service; all of these Services together are sometimes collectively referred to here as the Service known as "Google Music." Your usage of Google Music on or through Android devices will also be governed by our Mobile Privacy Policy, and your purchase history from the Google Music Store will also governed by the Google Wallet Privacy Policy, except where expressly modified by this Google Music Privacy Policy. In addition to the above, the following describes our additional privacy practices specific to your use of all aspects of Google Music. Capitalized terms not otherwise defined have the meanings given to them in the Google Music Terms.

Personal Information

  • Account information. Other than for browsing in the Google Music Store, you must have a Google Account that is linked to a Google Wallet Account to use Google Music, and because Google Music integrates access to your purchases and free content from the Google Music Store, your Google Music Social Recommendations, and with Your Music in Google Music Storage, you must use the same, identical Google Account for each component of Google Music, except for the Google Music Artist Hub. If you use any component of the Google Music Services, you will become automatically enabled to use the other components as well (except for the Google Music Artist Hub) using the same log-in credentials and Google Account. Google asks for some personal information when you create a Google Account, including your email address and password, which is used to protect your account from unauthorized access. If you do not already have a Google Wallet account, you will be required to create one and provide and to provide credit card and personal billing information. That billing information will be used to complete your purchases from the Google Music Store and, for security and geographical eligibility purposes, to verify your billing address. Google's servers automatically record certain information about your use of Google Music. Similar to other web services, Google records information such as account activity (e.g., storage usage, number of log-ins, actions taken), data displayed or accessed, and other logs information (e.g., browser or device type, IP address, date and time of access, cookie ID, and so forth).

  • Communications and Newsletters. In order to keep you informed about Google Music, we might send Service announcements and mandatory transactional information to the email address you provided when you registered for your Google Account. We might also offer you the ability to opt in to receive additional information and promotional offers related to Google's digital music and entertainment offerings more generally (such as Google Music Magnifier or similar items) or offers and information from our content or promotional partners; in each instance, you will only receive this information if you opt in to receive it, and you can change your options in the settings for your Google Music Account. You might also submit information to us when contacting us with a support question, and we might retain those emails or communications history for purpose of providing you with ongoing support or for purposes of internal training and product improvement.

  • Social Sharing. All information you share in public areas, such as through Google Music Social Recommendations, will be publicly visible to those who have access to your Google+ activity stream. This information will include your name as you have chosen to display it in your Google+ Profile, information about the song or album you have chosen to share with your friends on Google+, and whatever written message or text you choose to share with your friends in conjunction with your Google Music Social Recommendation.

  • Content and usage information. Google Music stores, processes, and maintains information about your usage, access, and playback of Your Music in Google Music Storage ("Personal Usage"); about your purchase history and playback activity related to items you preview and buy in the Google Music Store ("Store Usage"); and about the songs you share and listen to in connection with Google Music Social Recommendations ("Social Usage") (all forms of usage history together, your "Usage Activity"). We may use your Usage Activity as a whole in order to provide and personalize the Service to you (e.g., to build Instant Mix playlists at your request, to make personalized recommendations for future purchases in the Google Music Store, etc.), and to improve Google's products and services. Usage Activity may also include storage and analysis of information related to your use of the Service overall, playback counts, playlist content, and related information needed to provide you with personalized features of the Service. You can find more information about Instant Mix and personalized recommendations in the Google Music Help Center. We might also share de-identified data (i.e., without any personally identifiable information attached) derived from or reflecting your Store Usage and Social Usage to our content partners for purposes of sales and financial audits, analysis of trends and usage patterns, and in connection with reports and payments of royalties to artists and copyright owners. For purchases where we act as the content partner's agent, we include the state, city, and zip code from a purchaser's billing address in our de-identified transactions reports related to Store Usage.

  • Required Metadata from Record Labels Some purchases from the Google Music Store do not currently, but will in coming months, contain a unique identifier embedded in the metadata of digital download files that is required by some record labels of all companies that sell downloads of its music, known as "UITS" tags. While Google does not currently attach this metadata to download files, it is retaining transaction information sufficient to insert that metadata for already made purchases retroactively when we do begin using the required UITS tags. The metadata consists of a random number generated by Google using an algorithm to encrypt another algorithmically generated number that identifies the transaction for the purchase of that file, along with an identification of Google as the music store distributor and Google's digital signature, datestamp and/or timestamp of the purchase, and industry codes identifying the sound recording, and a digital indicator of whether the file has been modified. Google does not insert any personally identifiable information into this metadata.

  • Authorized Devices In order to authenticate your upload computers and/or authorized devices for offline access, to implement Service limitations as described in the Google Music Terms, and to enforce certain security limits related to contractual contractual commitments to content owners who license us music to sell, we may record device identifiers including your computer's MAC address or device identifiers such as IMEI or MEID for mobile devices. You can delete a device identifier when you wish to stop using and de-register that device.

Uses

We use your information to deliver the Service to you, to enforce usage limitations in connection with the Service, to process and personalize your requests in connection with your use of the Service, to provide non-personally identifiable usage data, transaction data, and trends to our content partners, to provide aggregate and/or de-identified usage data for copyright holders in royalty reports, to develop new features, and to improve the overall quality of Google's products and services.

Your choices

You may terminate your use of Google Music at any time by ceasing to use the Services (or any individual Service, such as Google Music Storage, Google Music Software, and so forth) and uninstalling any software related to the Service you wish to terminate. You may also delete Content (including all or any portion of Your Music or any purchased music from the Google Music Store) at any time and the Content and any data directly attached to it (e.g., stored play counts) will be deleted from our servers. There may be some delay before any deletion is reflected in our system. Previously gathered Usage Activity may not be deleted with your Content but can be deleted by terminating your Google Account; however, you will not be able to delete the records of your purchase transactions (including song or album purchased) from your Google Wallet account history. In addition, any information, statistics, and analysis derived from any Usage Activity, including de-identified Usage Activity that has already been shared with the parties and for the purposes listed above, will not be deleted but will remain de-identified and unconnected with your personally identifiable information.
